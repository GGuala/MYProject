ABout ME

I'm Nicolás Guala an artist, designer, and tech enthusiast. My practice today focuses mainly on digital sculpting and 3D printing. I'm interested in creating experiences where the viewer becomes an active participant, because I believe real art emerges when it is shaped and transformed by those who engage with it.

WOrks

Ethos

(2025)

Ethos explores divinity through sculptural forms that impose themselves in space. The hand, as the maker’s tool, and the face, as a mirror of the other, anchor a dialogue between body and spirit. These forms refuse beginnings or endings; they break, branch, and fold into themselves, challenging linearity and certainty.

The work captures spirituality as an unconscious force, creating itself in the moment, fragile yet resonant. Ethos becomes a meditation on becoming vibrating at the threshold between silence and eruption, presence and transcendence.

Cementerio

(2024)

Cementerio is a 3D-printed sculptural installation where each piece becomes a fossil of emotion grief, nostalgia, tenderness once intangible, now fixed in fragile, metallic forms resting on ash.

It is both personal and collective: a quiet anatomy of memory that transforms inner landscapes into matter, inviting reflection on what we cannot release yet refuse to forget.

Estacion N3

(2024)

Estación N3 is a participatory installation where viewers become creators. Around a communal table with tools, tape, and sculptural elements, participants cut, glue, paint, and play their gestures layering into a shared composition.

The work dissolves authorship, transforming spectators into collaborators. Creation becomes collective and alive, an open ritual of experimentation where the process itself is the artwork.

Mónada

(2023)

Mónada is a conceptual installation that explores duality and spiritual transformation. In dialogue with Entidad, it reflects on the contradictions of existence chaos and stillness, matter and spirit, routine and revelation.

The work centers on personal metamorphosis: from the burning flame within to the search for meaning in daily rituals. It suggests that divinity emerges not in transcendence, but in the lightness hidden within ordinary moments.
